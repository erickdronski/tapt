Tapt Community Guidelines

Last updated: [DATE, to be completed]

Tapt is where drinkers log real pours, vote on real beers, and find real spots. These guidelines keep it that way. They apply to everything you post: check-ins, ratings, votes, photos, notes, handles, and profiles. They are part of our Terms of Service.

1. Tapt is for adults

You must be of legal drinking age where you live (21+ in the United States). Do not post content that shows or encourages underage drinking, and do not help anyone under legal drinking age use Tapt.

2. Keep it real

  • Log beers you actually drank. Rate what you actually tasted.
  • No fake accounts, bought votes, or coordinated vote rings. The Beer Market runs on real votes, and we remove fabricated activity when we find it.
  • If you work for a brewery or venue, that is welcome, but do not pose as a neutral drinker to pump your own beers. Venues have their own channel at Tapt for Business.

3. Be decent

  • No harassment, hate, threats, or bullying. Criticize beers all you want; do not go after people.
  • No posting anyone's private information (addresses, phone numbers, and the like).
  • No spam, scams, or off-platform promotion dressed up as check-ins.
  • No sexually explicit content and no content that is illegal where you or we operate.

4. Drink responsibly

Never drink and drive, and do not glorify it here. Do not post content that celebrates dangerous overconsumption. If you or someone you know needs help, in the U.S. call SAMHSA's National Helpline at 1-800-662-4357.

5. Only post what is yours to post

Post your own photos and words. Do not upload images or text you do not have the right to share. Beer labels appearing incidentally in your own photo of your own pour are fine.

6. Reporting and blocking

  • Report: every piece of community content in Tapt can be reported from the app (long-press or the ••• menu on a post). You can also email hello@taptbeer.com.
  • Block: you can block any user from their post or profile. Blocked users disappear from your feed immediately.
  • Our commitment: we review reports of objectionable content and act within 24 hours, removing the content and ejecting the user who posted it where warranted.

7. What happens when we act

  • Depending on severity, we may remove content, warn the account, suspend it, or ban it. Fabricated voting and harassment lean toward the ban end.
  • Removed content stays out of public rankings and feeds.
  • Appeals: if you think we got it wrong, email hello@taptbeer.com from your account email and we will take a second look.

9. Venue and beer info

Partner menus are published by the venues themselves. Crowd-reported sightings can go stale. If something on a menu or map is wrong, report it in the app so we can fix it. Do not deliberately submit false venue or menu information.
