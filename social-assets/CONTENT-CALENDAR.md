# Tapt — Instagram content system (@taptbeerapp)

Everything is on the brand master (`brand/master.html` → `brand/out/brand-master.png`).
Voice: plain, confident, never salesy. No "real data" flex, no number/stat flex, no hype, no em dashes.
All facts are real. Handle **@taptbeerapp**, site **taptbeer.com**.

## Content pillars
1. **Who we are** — the app, what it does (identity)
2. **Taste & Feel** — a beer decoded (real BJCP sensory profile)
3. **Beer School / History** — education + fun facts
4. **The Beer Market** — beers rise/fall on votes
5. **For breweries & taprooms** — the B2B value
6. **Community** — polls, this-or-that, questions (mostly Stories)

### fact-1 — IPA / boat

> Learn every style in Tapt → taptbeer.com 🍺

### fact-2 — Guinness
> Did you know Guinness pours like it's going backwards? It's the nitrogen. Bubbles get pushed down the sides of the glass and rise up the middle, so the settle looks reversed.
> taptbeer.com 🍺
>
> #guinness #stout #beerfacts #beer #craftbeer #beereducation

### fact-3 — lager vs ale
> Beer school: lager vs ale isn't about color. It's the yeast. Ale ferments warm and fruity up top; lager ferments cold and clean at the bottom. Both come pale or dark.
> taptbeer.com 🍺
>
> #beerschool #beereducation #craftbeer #beer #lager #ale

### fact-4 — Pilsner
> The first golden beer is younger than you'd think. Pilsner was born in Plzeň in 1842. Most beer before it was dark. Clear glass finally showed the color off.
> taptbeer.com 🍺
>
> #pilsner #beerhistory #beer #craftbeer #beerfacts #lager

### fact-5 — stout
> "Stout" just meant strong. A stout porter was simply a bigger porter. Over time the dark, roasty beer kept the name all for itself.
> taptbeer.com 🍺
>
> #stout #beerhistory #beerschool #beer #craftbeer #beerfacts
